Design Dilemma

The client needed a logo for this new and developing organization: Women in Government Contracting (WiGC). As the details of the organization itself were still being finalized, they had no direction to give with regards to their vision for the new logo. Ready. Set. Go.

Reasoning & Process

It’s always the most difficult to be creative with no guidelines—if the client doesn’t know what they want, then I have to assess their needs and surmise their desires myself.

Do they want just the acronym in the logo or do they want all the text or both?

They don’t know if they have a preference here, so I’ll design one of each variant.

What is their target audience?

This organization is geared toward women who work in government contracts with a focus on providing connections, etc., to build their success.

What kind of company is this?

WiGC is a developing sub-organization of Wiley Rein geared specifically toward women in contract law. WiGC provides opportunities for networking, mentoring, sharing knowledge, and professional growth with the goal of advancing the success of its members.

WiGC is organized by Wiley Rein, a corporate law firm located in downtown Washington DC. Wiley Rein’s branding is fairly modest and professional, with a subdued and classy color scheme.

What does their competition look like?

Fortunately, they had done some research into similar organizations already and were able to provide me with links to their websites so I could see how they presented themselves visually.

Keeping all of this, and the look and feel of the Wiley Rein brand, in mind, I broke out paper and a pen and started by making a list of ideas (elements of the Wiley Rein logo that I could reference, keywords relating to law, etc.) and then started sketching out concepts by hand. I wanted to keep the WiGC logo relatable to the Wiley Rein logo/brand since the organizations are associated, but allow the WiGC logo to have its own independent identity. The path I chose was to keep the color schemes the same and make subtle references to the Wiley Rein logo.

I also continued doing research online to see what other people were doing with these keywords. When I had some good concepts, I moved my sketching off of paper and into Illustrator. There, I started mocking things up in different variations. I printed out a bunch and narrowed them down to three different logos which I submitted to WiGC/Wiley Rein.

Solution

Logo Solution A

This logo utilizes both the Bauer Bodoni & News Gothic typefaces, and a palette of colors taken from the Wiley Rein style guide. The work “women” is the biggest point size, a bold weight, and the only text in green, therefore emphasizing the word: appropriate since this group is all about empowering women specifically. The word “in” is set to be just shorter than the “n” in “women” and is colored the same as “government contracting” so as not to detract from the word “women” even though they are on the same line and to further tie the top line and bottom line of text in together. Setting “government contracting” in all caps further distinguishes the word “women” and also allows “government contracting” to additionally be its own entity within the logo. The squares are a general reference to the Wiley Rein logo. Having multiple squares in close, though varied, proximity to one another symbolizes networking. More specifically, having a large, dark green square as the anchor of the squares element is a direct reference to the dark green square in the Wiley Rein logo: symbolizing that Wiley Rein is the anchor of this group (i.e., that Wiley Rein is the parent of WiGC, that Wiley Rein is facilitating the networking happening at WiGC events).

Logo Solution B

This logo utilizes the Bauer Bodoni typeface and the two Wiley Rein greens. In a conservative, minimalist intention, the acronym is simply laid out in a straight line, with a thin, lighter green line above and below as a loose reference to the square of the Wiley Rein logo. The idea is to create a related abstraction of elements from the Wiley Rein logo which allow the WiGC logo to be its own entity, but to coordinate with the Wiley Rein logo without being as bold, so as to further emphasize that WiGC is an independent sub-group of Wiley Rein.

Logo Solution C

This logo utilizes News Gothic typeface and a varied color palette taken from the Wiley Rein style guide. This logo marries the acronym to the written-out title side-by-side, to echo the rectangular shape of the outline around “WiGC” creating further harmony between the two. The “w” is a dark green and bold, emphasizing the importance of women: appropriate since this group is all about empowering women specifically. This logo is a little more playful, while still remaining simple and professional: setting the “c” inside of the “g” which not only echoes the round shape of both letters, but further relates the words “government” and “contracting” in a literal, visual way. The entire acronym is contained within a rectangle, which serves to accentuate both the larger, general shape that “WiGC” creates and the rectangular shape of the logo in its’ entirety. Placing “women in government contracting” to the right of the rectangle provides an immediate translation of the acronym and coloring that text green provides a balance to the heavy green “w” on the far left. This logo remains unified, even though it is composed of two distinct halves.
