Design Dilemma

The client requested some custom graphics for use across multiple projects: a few (fake) company logos, various iconic graphics, and an e-mail graphic.

Reasoning & Process

Logos

The pseudonym logos had no particular direction, but I thought that it might be useful to create some that seemed related to the field of the logo that they were replacing, so that relevant references in the text seemed in-line with the appearance of the logo. “Tesla Electronics” was created to replace any sort of electronic company, “green cycle” to replace any environmentally-friendly or recycling company, and Kespo was specifically created to be a little more generic looking, so that it could be used in a wider variety of circumstances.

Icons

Some icons were created in color, and some were created specifically in grayscale for a selection of documents that would be printed in black and white. With all icons, my first objective was to make sure that the style and color matched with the style and color of the existing icon library that they would become a part of. Keeping that in mind, it was then about distilling the idea that the icon was to represent into a simplified form that was easily and clearly recognizable. The process for this involved drawing a simplified image and then seeing if anything further could be removed without losing the intent of the idea being represented.

Solution

The logos, icons, and graphics were all merged into their respective libraries at Corporate Executive Board. They are used in a variety of materials.
