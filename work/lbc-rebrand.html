Design Dilemma

The client needed a visual identity that better communicated the services of the business on its online presence (currently, exclusively facebook.)

Reasoning & Process

Butterflies weren’t helping to show off the business and the script text was hard to read—especially in the profile picture. As always, when starting from scratch, questions first:

What kind of company is this?

Laurie’s Basket Case is a home-based small business selling pre-made and customized gift baskets.

What are the products like?

There are many varieties of gift baskets being sold. They are all very colorful and fun—this should be communicated with the brand. Keywords: color, fun, and variety/categories.

Get out the pen and paper! I started by taking inventory of all the listed types of gift baskets on LBC’s facebook page. I came up with the concept of a flat-color design, utilizing a custom icon library displayed in white over blocks of color to both further unify the icons as a set and to visually highlight the variety in the products. After sketching many examples, I narrowed the list down to a solid 12 icons.

The logo would be in the same color pallet, and would also be a flat-color design depicting a fun, iconic gift basket. I chose a bold font for the bulk of the name to reflect the large, flat color shapes. Typesetting “Laurie’s” in a script font is a visual reference to the personalized nature of the business.

I decided on a bright color pallet to be eye-catching and fun. Since I was primarily designing this image for facebook, I laid down a 4x10 grid of squares to serve as the backdrop of the cover photo and began experimenting with color placement within that framework. Once I was satisfied with the composition, phase two was to decide which blocks got an icon, and which icons they were, taking into account which squares would be hidden behind facebook elements.

Solution

The branding much more accurately represents the business; the bright color pallet conveys the fun nature of her products, and the icons show diversity while remaining a unified library of elements. These components could also be easily rearranged or reversed (having the icons be in the various palette colors on a white background) if the background needed to feel less busy in another application, for example. This new branding will easily translate to other mediums (i.e. business cards, a website) as the business grows. Laurie was pleased with the final results. You can see her facebook page here.
