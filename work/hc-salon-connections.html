Design Dilemma

Salon Connections is an internal magazine for Hair Cuttery that is distributed to stylists working in Hair Cuttery salons around the country. In addition to making edits and small design changes throughout the publication, there were still two pages that needed to be created from scratch. There wasn’t much guidance for the appearance of the pages, just that they should look like pages found in other fashion magazines. Aaannd, go!

Reasoning & Process

My first priority was to make sure that the new pages tied back into the magazine, so I pulled recurring elements from other pages (such as the pink-to-purple splotches) onto my artboard. I flipped through a bunch of beauty and fashion magazines for layout idea inspiration.

Styling Must-Haves

Since the “Styling Must Haves” page was meant to highlight a few different products, I thought that grouping them together and having the related text arrayed around the central image would be a good solution after sketching out a few different options on paper. I resized and edited the individual product images and combined them into a pleasing array for the center of the page. This was a perfect place to add the circular splotchy element used in other pager right behind, to both tie this page into the publication and to further group the products together as a set. Once I got all of the text laid out in a way that fit well into the layout, I created an arrow element to connect the individual product to its appropriate descriptor. Once the layout was finalized, I integrated a few other design elements into the page to both tie in the page and break up some of the larger areas of white space.

Foursquare

The “Foursquare” page was very much about the app (and the current deal) so I tried to keep the focus on that by using large images and minimal text. I pulled in some of the reusable pink-to-purple elements for this page as well as using the same type of arrow from the “Styling Must Haves” page.

Solution

Both pages tied in well to the rest of the publication due to the repeated usage of elements implemented throughout. Neither page is static: both have a clear hierarchy, a good balance of white space, and visual interest unique to themselves that fits within the larger scope of the magazine.
